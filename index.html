My Favorite Places to Eat in New Orleans

Sukho Thai

2200 Royal St.

My favorite Thai restaurant in the city. I like spicy food, but they're on a higher scale of spiciness. When they ask how hot you want it from 1 - 5 I think 2 is good and hot (I swear I'm not a total spice wimp!)

Favorite Dishes:

  • Drunken Noodles
  • Red Curry
  • Kaffir Lime Curry Tofu

Bennachin

1212 Royal St.

Really good African cuisine with influences from The Gambia and Cameroon. As the chef Bryant Terry noted, a lot of what we think of as Southern food in the US has roots in African cuisine, so the food here isn't so unfamiliar. In some ways there is more of a veggie focus than in your typical Southern US food, which is nice.

Favorite Dishes:

  • Black Eyed Pea Fritters
  • Fried Plantains
  • Coconut Rice w/ Peas and Corn

Cafe Abyssinia

3511 Magazine St.

Delicious and well-spiced Ethiopian food with really good sour spongy injera flatbread to eat it with.

Favorite Dishes:

  • Misir Wot - Spicy Red Lentils
  • Sambusas
  • Injera Bread

Mopho

514 City Park Ave.

Vietnamese-Louisianian fusion restaurant, highly acclaimed by local chefs in particular. New Orleans actually has a strong Vietnamese culture and some Vietnamese food has a common French influence with New Orleans Creole food. The "po-mi" is a play on words combining the New Orleans po-boy and Vietnamese bahn-mi, which are similar kinds of sandwiches.

  • Crispy Tofu Vindaloo Bowl
  • Cast Iron Roast Tofu "Po-Mi"
  • Veggie Pho
